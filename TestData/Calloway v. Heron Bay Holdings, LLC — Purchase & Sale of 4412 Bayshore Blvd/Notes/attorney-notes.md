**Matter No.:** RE-2026-0418  |  **Lead:** A. Vega, Esq.  |  **Paralegal:** (internal)

### Posture
We represent the **buyer, Marcus Calloway**. Seller (**Heron Bay Holdings, LLC**, managing member **Diane Ruelle**) repudiated by email refusing to set a closing table. We intend to file for **specific performance** in the 13th Circuit, Hillsborough County. Earnest money of **$63,750** is held by **Gulfstream Title & Escrow, LLC** (escrow file **GT-58821**).

### Key tension
- The **PSA (§7.2)** fixes the financing/inspection contingency deadline at **March 14, 2026** and the closing date at **April 10, 2026**.
- The seller's agent's **email of March 9** asserts the closing date is **March 31, 2026** — this **contradicts the executed PSA**. Run this contradiction to ground; it is the crux of the repudiation defense.

### Hidden / non-obvious items to confirm
- The **scanned inspection report** appears clean on a quick skim, but OCR pulls a buried line noting an **active roof leak above the second-floor commercial unit (Unit 2C)** — a material defect the seller never disclosed. This only shows on OCR. (Planted fact #1.)
- The **closing statement spreadsheet** lists a **$4,800 'undisclosed lien payoff — Sunstate Code Enforcement'** in the debits column that nobody flagged in correspondence. Tie this to the title commitment Schedule B-II exceptions. (Planted fact #2.)

### Research targets
- FL specific-performance standard for land-sale contracts; adequacy of remedy at law; statute of frauds compliance (PSA is signed & definite). See *DK Arena v. EB Acquisitions*.
- Public-records angle: opposing counsel hinted the county may treat the recorded contract/lis pendens materials as public records — confirm under *Microdecisions v. Skinner* whether the property appraiser's GIS/records carry any copyright restriction. Likely a red herring but address it.
